Netting

The volatility of a portfolio is lower than the sum of the volatility of the underlying assets due to the correlation effect. Consider a portfolio of 2 assets that are 100% negatively correlated. There will be zero risk while this correlation is in effect. A portfolio of 2 assets that are 100% positively correlated will generally have twice the volatility of either). For a 2 asset portfolio, the variance can be measured as A2 + ρAB + B2 where A and B are the volatilities and ρ is the correlation which ranges from -1 to +1.
